“A Prescription for Death”

The number of deaths caused by preventable errors at hospitals is already worrying. But patient deaths caused by a doctor’s bad handwriting is downright shameful.
Medication mistakes kill more than 7,000 people in the US every year, mainly due to the illegible handwriting of many doctors, according to a July 2006 report issued by the National Academies of Science's Institute of Medicine (IOM).
One big reason could be time- the lack of it. Doctors work in a largely volume-driven business, where they’re expected to do more in less time. Many doctors claim that their terrible handwriting has never caused them or their patients any problems, and that their patients call them up when they or their pharmacists can’t understand what the prescription says. But it’s high time they realize it’s better to be safe than sorry.
